Conceptual Response

I created a prototype called Emotion Dice, is an interactive web that users can roll a dice to trigger a piece of music that represents a specific emotion, users not just directly control what they want to listen, but they explore the music through a creative way with randomness and playfulness. By connecting the dice with emotions, this prototype becomes like a self-discover tool, the randomness mirrors the unpredictability of human emotions themselves, users can feel engaging through interact and experience the different emotions of music. This prototype follows the creative prompt of skeuomorphism, the design makes users feel like they are playing with the real dice, which let the interface intuitive and evoke emotional engagement through the experience.

Design References

Interface Reference

For this prototype, I been inspired by the casino dice. When watching the dice roll it creates a sense of anticipation and excitement, which I also want to bring out the unpredictability feeling throughout my prototype. One similar project I found is called Dice Virtu World, this web shows a digital dice roll that will randomly generates various emotions, just like my prototype, but this web more focus on textual prompt, however, my design extends the concept in music and sounds, which users can be more engaging through the experience and makes the prototype more playful and interesting.

Visual Plan & Sketch

For the visual planning of the prototype, I sketched the dice faces those present different emotions with minimalist and cartoon-like style, which let the users feel friendly and characterful. Through the simple face icon can enhances the readability, let the users can recognize the expressions even with small size.

Technical Approach

The main technique I utilized for my prototype was Math.random, in order to simulate the unpredictability of rolling dice. When users click the Roll the Dice button, this Math.random will generate a random number from 0 to 5, which is an index to the faces array that includes all the dice images and their related music audios. Therefore, when the result is selected, the dice face shows up and automatically playing the corresponding music. To simulate the movement of the rolling dice, the prototype using setInterval() to cycle the dice faces rapidly before it stop, creates a sense of playfulness. The audio elements and volume slider allow users to control the output of the sounds to present the cohesive system of this design.
